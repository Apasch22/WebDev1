Engines

There are quite a few different types of engines found in cars. The engines we will go over are the V engine, inline engine and boxer engine.

What do engines do?

The engine's job is to make your car move by converting gas into motion. Engines found in cars are internal combustion engines, which usually follow the four-stroke cycle.

  1. Intake: The intake valves opens and a mixture of fuel and air go into the combustion chamber.
  2. Compression: The intake valves close and the piston moves up and compresses the air and fuel.
  3. Combustion: A spark plug fires to help the mixture combust, forcing the piston to move back down.
  4. Exhaust: The exhaust valves open and the exhaust gases are pushed out by the piston.

Note that this explanation doesn't perfectly apply to all engines, such diesel engines for example, which doesn't need spark plugs.

The V Engine

V engines got their name because they're shaped like a V. The engine's cylinder banks are set up in a V shape creating either a 60 or 90 degree angle. The first V engine was created by Wilhelm Mayback in 1889.

The Inline Engine

Inline engines (also known as straight engines) are engines where all of the pistons and cylinders are set up in a straight row. Inline engines are usually more balanced and quiter than V engines, but have a higher center of gravity.

The Boxer Engine

Boxer engines are a type of flat engine, so they have pistons that are parallel to the ground. However, it's important to note that not every flat engine is a boxer engine. The boxer engine gets its name because the motion of the pistons resemble the motion of a boxer punching. Pistons are attached to a crank pin so pairs of pistons move inverse to each other. For example, when one piston goes out, the piston on the other side moves out as well. Boxer engines have better balance and increase fluid rotation.
